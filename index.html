    21st Oct - 25th Oct Key Takeaways:

  • Bond yields going up suggest the Fed rate cut was a policy mistake
    • Yields are expected to go down after a rate cut cause existing bond prices suddenly look more appealing with a higher yield
    • The fact yields are going up, and bond prices going down, means investors would rather not buy the existing bonds because they are not confident in their creditworthiness, which is concerning
    • Why is it concerning? Because it means the market believes the Fed’s decision in cutting rates was not a good one. Interest rate cuts are supposed to facilitate consumer borrowing. But if yields go high, especially 10-year bonds, then interest rates for non-durable goods are actually rising.
    • If that happens then the economy will slow down which is the opposite of what the Fed attempted to do in the first place.
      • What can the Fed do next?
        • QE. Print more money and buy these bonds.
        • This creates even more US deficit
  • A broad sell-off could indicate that investors expect higher future inflation or interest rates. If inflation expectations rise, the real return on bonds (after adjusting for inflation) diminishes, making them less attractive.
  • If investors think the Fed will raise rates in the future to combat inflation or other risks, they might sell bonds in anticipation of lower bond prices later.
    • This creates a doom loop:
      • Economy improves -> fed cuts rates to lower inflation -> but inflation exp rise -> yields go up -> Fed does QE or rate hike -> economy improves -> fed cuts rates (and so on)
  • Other than the above, this week is slow, right after OPEX. Market is unpinned and will be more volatile. All eyes on the election (Trump odds leading SPX) and $8B in corporate buybacks starting soon.
